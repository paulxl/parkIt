Resources

For Explorers

Gear

Amazon

Speaks for itself- largest inventory available and an excellent return policy for all things sold and fulfilled by Amazon. Hundreds of reviews on almost all gear.

Backcountry

Their fantastic Learning Resources page can help you to choose and use your gear. Any unused gear can be returned for a full refund at any time.

Backcountry Gear

Specializing in lightweight gear for thru-hiking, Backcountry Gear is a great place to find the lightest and strongest gear for expert hikers.

Eastern Mountain Sports

Massive selection of top brands, but even better, they'll accept returns on unused or defective gear for a full refund within a year of purchase.

The House

Free shipping on orders over $50 and unused returns accepted back within 30 days for credit or exchange.

Moosejaw

Popular retailer that sells all major brands and accepts returns at any time as long as the item is in good enough condition to be resold.

REI

REI has been selling outdoor gear for almost 100 years, so you can trust they know a thing or two when it comes to getting equipped. Returns are accepted on items within a year of purchase.

Planning

AllTrails

Great place for planning an adventure! AllTrails allows you to create, find, log, and share your favorite trails. You can even create of todo list (or bucket list!) of any trails you find that must be traveled.

Hiking Project

In a similar vein to AllTrails, but they have a great trail directory where you can see all the trails for a given geographical area. They also have a fantastic mobile app that provides you with 3D trail maps on the go.

National Park Service

Who better to give you information on the parks than the people who run them? The NPS website is an exceptional point of information where you can get real time trail and park status (like closures, weather events, etc).

Washington Trails Association

A 501(c) nonprofit that specializes in educating and empowering. Not only can you find the latest up-to-date on all trails in Washington state, but you can also sign up to volunteer for activities such as trail maintenance.

APIs

Hiking Project Data API

Hiking Project provides a simple API with access to certain limited data. All of the data returned by the API is already available on publicly available pages on the Hiking Project site. Returned data is json.

Mapbox

An open source mapping platform for custom designed maps.

Washington Geospatial Open Data Portal

An API on park accommodations for visitors to Washington State Parks (Restroom facilities, kitchen shelters, picnic tables, ranger stations and so forth.)

Washington State Department of Transportation

An API for travel times, weather information, and mountain pass conditions in Washington State.
